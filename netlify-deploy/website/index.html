Craftsmanship

Made by hand, not by machine

Each Origin begins as flat hides selected for grain quality and consistency. The leather is cut, the pocket moulds are formed, and the internal structure is shaped before any stitching begins.

Hand-stitching is not a decorative choice. It creates a stronger, more resilient join than machine stitching — and if a single stitch ever fails, the rest hold firm. It also allows the maker to feel the tension in each pull, adjusting for the natural variation in the leather.

The entire process — from hide selection to finished product — takes place in our Melbourne workshop. No outsourcing. No assembly line. One maker, one object, start to finish.

The maker

Curiosity about what a material could become

The Tarrant Collective was founded by a designer and maker whose relationship with materials began long before any formal training. As a child, she was deeply tactile — often found rolling a stone in her hand, making umbrellas out of ferns, pressing bark into sheets not yet knowing why. Only that it had to be tried.

That bark did not make fabric. But it made the base for paint. Failure, it turned out, was just the beginning of something else.

Self-taught across painting, moulding and leatherwork, everything made under the Tarrant name starts the same way: with curiosity about what a material could become in the hands.

The name Tarrant carries a family lineage. It is chosen carefully, because everything here is.
