# The Tarrant Collective — Brand Document

## 01 — Brand Identity

### Official Brand Name

The Tarrant Collective

### Brand Story

The Tarrant Collective was founded by a designer and maker whose relationship with materials began long before any formal training. As a child, she was deeply tactile — often found rolling a stone in her hand, making umbrellas out of ferns, pressing bark into sheets not yet knowing why — only that it had to be tried.

That bark did not make fabric. But it made the base for paint. Failure, it turned out, was just the beginning of something else.

Self-taught across painting, moulding and leatherwork, everything made under the Tarrant name starts the same way: with curiosity about what a material could become in the hands. The name Tarrant carries a family lineage. It is chosen carefully, because everything here is.

## 02 — The Product

### Product Description

The Tarrant — Origin is the first in its story. Hand-stitched leather that exists in its own category. Not a bag. Not a tool. Slim, deliberate, and precisely engineered — it holds every essential a modern professional carries, and turns over into a flat working surface.

Made for the intentional person who values well-made objects and carries them with purpose.

### Key Features & Specifications

- Full-grain leather construction, hand-stitched with contrast thread detail on darker colourways
- Moulded internal pocket structure — a precision-engineered carry system, not just stitched leather
- D-ring carry hardware with strap
- Turns over to become a flat working surface
- Slim, architectural profile
- Holds: laptop, stylus/pen, iPhone, AirPods, charger, keys, glasses

### Colourways

Three colourways at launch:

1. **Midnight** — Deep rich black, silver hardware, contrast white stitching
2. **Slate** — Complex cool charcoal, subtle and architectural
3. **Natural** — Raw pale leather, gold hardware, extraordinary visible grain

### Use Cases

- Daily commute
- Business trips
- Laptop and work essentials
- Design on the run
- Client meetings
- Work on the go
- Onsite design consultations

### Price Point

Mid-range premium positioning: **$450–$600 AUD** retail.

### Future Products

The Tarrant Collective is a studio with multiple product concepts in development across different categories and materials — not limited to leather.

## 03 — Target Customer

### Ideal Customer

The Tarrant customer is not defined by age or profession — they are defined by sensibility.

They buy with intention. They notice when something is well made. They would rather own one right thing than three adequate things.

In practice: architects, designers, executives, creatives, hospitality professionals, corporate HR managers, students, freelancers. They carry devices daily and are frustrated that nothing on the market carries all their essentials elegantly.

### Customer Psychographics

- Buy fewer, better things
- Design-conscious with an eye for detail
- Early adopter mentality
- Intuitive understanding of point of difference
- Values authenticity and longevity
- The intuitive collector — for those who know

### Geographic Market

- **Phase 2:** International expansion (US, UK, Europe, Asia)

## 06 — Design & Brand Aesthetic

### Mood

- Modern luxury
- Understated elegance
- Contemporary and refined

The product is more architectural and precise than purely warm/craft. The site should feel like the meeting point between artisan making and modern professional design — not rustic, not cold.

### Colour Palette

| Role | Colour | Hex |
|------|--------|-----|
| Primary | Near-black | `#0F0F12` |
| Primary | Deep charcoal | `#1A1A1F` |
| Accent | Gold | `#C4A072` |
| Supporting | Warm off-white | `#E8E4DE` |
| Supporting | Natural leather tone | `#E8E0D4` |

**Backgrounds:** Dark throughout for hero and product sections. Warm cream/off-white for story and text-heavy sections.

**Never:** Cold grey, clinical white, blue tones.

### Typography

**Approach:** Serif headings, sans-serif body.

**Headings:** Georgia, Playfair Display, or similar — heritage character that suggests lineage without being old-fashioned.

**Body:** Clean light sans-serif — Jost, Karla, or similar. Precision and space. Nothing that draws attention to itself.

### Design References

- **Aesop** (aesop.com) — brand story approach, sparse copy, product confidence
- **Bellroy** (bellroy.com) — carry product photography and feature explanation style
- **Leatherology** (leatherology.com) — leather goods presentation
- **Prestige Shopify theme** — editorial layout approach

### Aesthetic to Avoid

- Rustic or "handmade market" styling — the product is precise, not artisan-rough
- Fashion brand clichés — floating product, lots of white, lifestyle models in expensive locations
- Busy navigation or too many competing calls to action
- Cold greys or blues — the palette is warm-dark throughout
- Any layout that competes with the product image
- Generic bag/accessory brand aesthetics

### Guiding Principles

- The product does not fit neatly into a category. The site should never try to force it into one — not a bag brand, not a tech accessories brand, not a stationery brand. It is its own thing.
- The brand story is the most important asset on the site. The About / Brand Story page is not secondary — it is where the customer decides whether they trust and connect with this. It should be treated with the same importance as the product page.
- The Tarrant Collective is a studio, not just a product. The site architecture should allow for future products to be added cleanly without requiring a redesign.
- Everything should feel as considered as the object itself. If a design decision makes the site look like every other premium accessory brand, it is the wrong decision.
- The name Tarrant carries lineage and meaning. That weight should be felt throughout.
